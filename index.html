What is the Pomodoro Technique?

In a world filled with endless distractions, time management has become more important than ever. Learning to work with time (rather than against it) is one of the keys to mastering productivity. Thatâ€™s why Francesco Cirillo created the Pomodoro Technique in the late 1980s.

Widely popularized by news outlets and a barrage of apps and websites, the technique uses a timer to break down work into intervals called Pomodoros - aptly named after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer that Cirillo used as a university student. Each Pomodoro is traditionally 25 minutes in length, followed by a short break of 5 minutes. After the short break is complete, another Pomodoro is started. The cycle continues for four rounds, when the short break is replaced with a longer break of anywhere from 10-30 minutes.

What are the principles of the original technique?

Principles of the Pomodoro Technique:

  1. Decide on a task to accomplish
  2. Set the Pomodoro Timer (traditionally 25 minutes)
  3. Work on the task
  4. End work when the timer rings
  5. After four pomodoros, take a longer break (10-30 minutes)
  6. Repeat steps 1-5

What are the benefits of using the Pomodoro Technique?

The goal of the Pomodoro Technique is the limit the negative import of interruptions while focusing on work. Benefits of using the Pomodoro Technique include:

  • Mitigating interruptions while working
  • Reducing the total number of time spent on work by increasing concentration
  • Preventing burnout
  • Working together with time, instead of against it
  • Simplifying the complexity of achieving your goals
  • Optimizing task completion

Feel free to give the Pomodoro Technique a try and see the benefits for yourself!
